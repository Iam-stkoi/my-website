The Problem

Nigeria has the land.
The people. The hunger.
But the gap remains.

Nigeria imports billions of naira worth of rice every year — not because the soil is poor, but because the system is broken. Farmers can't get funded. Investors can't find farmland. Food prices keep rising.

This is not a farming problem. It is a connection problem. And IsaacVest is closing it.

Farmers have land, no capital

Smallholder farmers are shut out of bank loans. Without funding, they can't plant at scale — so Nigeria imports what it could grow.

Investors have capital, no access

Millions of Nigerians want to invest in real assets but have no trusted agricultural vehicle built for them.

No structured connector exists

There is no organized, transparent infrastructure linking capital to farmland at scale. Until now.

What We Do

Three Arms.
One Mission.

01
🌱
Crop Ownership Program

Invest a fixed amount and own a unit of farmland. IsaacVest manages everything — planting, growing, harvesting. You earn a share of the yield. No hoe required.

02
🍚
Rice Processing & Sales

We operate large-scale rice farms in Ogun State. Harvested rice is processed and sold to distributors, institutions, and markets — generating direct commercial revenue.

03
🔗
Farmer-Investor Marketplace

Local farmers register and list their farms. Verified investors choose who to fund. IsaacVest vets every farmer, structures every deal, and manages accountability — earning a transparent platform fee.

The Process

Simple. Transparent.
Built for Trust.

1

You Invest

Choose a crop unit or farmer project starting from ₦100,000. Transparent terms, clearly defined returns.

2

We Manage

IsaacVest handles every step — land, inputs, planting, growing, and harvesting. You receive regular progress reports.
